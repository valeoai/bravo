Overview

The BRAVO workshop aims at increasing awareness - and fostering progress on solutions - for the key challenge of trustworthy model validation on safety-critical systems deployed on open roads.

Advances in machine learning (ML) are bringing rise to highly automated (L2+ and, very recently, L3) advanced driving assistance systems (ADAS) in passenger cars, as well as autonomous vehicles (AVs) like robo-taxis, shuttles, and delivery droids. Such systems are about to revolutionize the services and ecosystem of mobility. Along with electrification, they aim at making the transportation of people and goods safer, more efficient, more inclusive, and more sustainable. Yet, deploying data-driven safety-critical systems to the open road, with limited onboard resources (power, compute, and memory) and long-lasting (10+ years) guarantees, is a formidable task.

For safe deployment in the wild, ADAS/AVs must cope with a wide range of driving conditions, including rare, dangerous situations and all sorts of severe perturbations, from sun glare and sensor impediment to adversarial attacks. Moreover, to secure certification by regulatory bodies - and the confidence of users - those capabilities must be provable. Unfortunately, ML still struggles with robustness, generalization power, transparency, and verification of models: even assessing those properties is difficult, for a lack of tools and test-beds.

The proliferation of public multi-sensor driving datasets (as the recent nuScenes and Waymo Open), as well as progresses in architecture design and training, will keep fostering improved in-the-lab performances of driving stacks. However, the community must also build tools to predict the actual, in-the-wild performance and robustness of systems. Accurate validation of existing models and systems is paramount for their societal acceptance and adoption.
